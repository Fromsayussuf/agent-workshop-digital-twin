Fluton is a confidentiality-first, intent-based cross-chain execution protocol that simplifies how users move assets and interact with decentralized applications across multiple blockchains. Instead of manually bridging, swapping, and managing complex multi-step transactions, users express what they want to achieve, and Fluton turns this into an encrypted intent. Independent relayers compete to fulfill the request by delivering the desired outcome on the destination chain, while the user’s funds remain securely locked and later used to repay the relayer through cross-chain settlement.

What makes Fluton fundamentally different is that confidentiality is built into the core of the system, not added as an afterthought. Intents, transaction parameters, and in advanced cases even token amounts and addresses can be handled in encrypted form, reducing information leakage and protecting user strategy, identity, and activity patterns. This enables a new model of private cross-chain execution where users can interact across ecosystems without exposing sensitive data.
